DADA

Dada was a movement from 1916 – 1923 that consisted of variety mediums; literature, poetry, song, dance, theatre, painting, photography, collage, mixed media, photomontage, sculpture, puppetry, ready-mades. It prompted new techniques and mediums, and rejected the traditional and the normal. Dada was anti-art, anti-aesthetic, and anti-war. It sought to reject the the "ideals" prevelant in the arts and society at the time

World War I raged through Europe. With the advent of the machine gun and rapid-fire field artillery gun and the use of tanks, warplanes and chemical weapons, there were massive casualties. Many artists and intellectuals sought refuge in Switzerland, as a neutral country. Hugo Ball was one of these artists. After arriving in Zurich, Ball founded Dada and established the Café Voltaire as a central gathering and performance space for the movement.
